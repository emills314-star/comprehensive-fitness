# Workout-science evidence ledger

## Scope and method

This ledger records the scientific basis that may legitimately influence recommendation logic. Evidence is separated from product judgment. The application remains informational fitness software, not a diagnostic or clinical prescription system.

The repository's current research scope is primarily healthy adult resistance training with a male-hypertrophy emphasis. Results from mixed-sex, untrained, athletic, older, or clinical populations are not silently treated as personalized facts about the user.

Strength labels used below:

- **High:** current position stand, overview of reviews, or convergent systematic reviews/meta-analyses with direct population/outcome relevance.
- **Moderate:** systematic synthesis with limited studies, indirect population/outcome, heterogeneity, or uncertain implementation precision.
- **Low:** single trial, mechanistic evidence, expert consensus, or product judgment without validated thresholds.

## Product-policy rules that must not be presented as settled science

- Exact 18-set session cap, 75/100-minute session targets, high-fatigue compound counts, spinal/grip score ceilings, and two-exercises-per-muscle limits.
- Exact 0.25/0.5 exercise-specific fractional credits beyond the validated canonical mapping confidence.
- Exact readiness ratios, staleness scores, score weights, confidence percentages, 35/56-day windows, and deload factors.
- Exact specialization and lower-fatigue volume multipliers.
- Predicted Program Effectiveness as an outcome guarantee.

These may remain transparent, tested product defaults when they improve practicality or safety. They must be versioned, explained as defaults, overrideable when safe, and evaluated against outcomes rather than described as personalized optima.

## Implementation invariants derived from evidence and scope

1. Never progress on current pain, invalid technique, missing required effort evidence, or materially incomplete prescribed work.
2. Never recommend unavailable equipment, an excluded exercise, a duplicate slot, or an unmapped canonical muscle.
3. Never let a soft score/preference override a hard safety, equipment, time, or explicit exclusion constraint.
4. Never fabricate personalization or a causal explanation from an unobserved field.
5. Preserve productive exercises; variation and deloading require a reason, not a calendar trigger alone.
6. Reduce session time coherently by priority/dose, not arbitrary truncation.
7. Distinguish goal-specific strength practice from hypertrophy dose distribution.
8. Treat readiness as same-day context; do not rewrite the base mesocycle or diagnose health status.
9. Expose evidence versus product judgment, missing data, applied constraints, and consequences of overrides.
10. Preserve historical recommendation snapshots and version all future rule changes.

## Known evidence limitations

- Many studies are short, supervised, and conducted in young healthy adults; trained lifters and long-term adherence are less certain.
- Sex-specific, older-adult, disability, pregnancy, pediatric, clinical, and sport-specific recommendations are outside the current product evidence scope.
- Exercise-muscle relationships combine anatomy, biomechanics, and expert judgment; exact per-exercise set credit is not directly validated for every individual.
- Research supports population-average directions, not exact predictions of one user's hypertrophy, recovery, or injury risk.
