Comprehensive Fitness is a local-first workout tracker. The app stores workout sessions, exercise names, sets, reps, weights, RPE, notes, and settings on your device.

Data Collection

The app does not require an account. Workout data is stored locally in IndexedDB. Rest notifications and workout cloud copy are separate optional controls. Enabling notifications sends only the installation-scoped push subscription and timer details needed to deliver requested alerts; it does not enable workout upload. Workout copies are uploaded only after you explicitly enable Optional workout cloud copy in Settings.

Private personal-evidence packages and backups are selected by you and validated locally before they replace local data. A hosted app does not probe private file paths. Automatic private-evidence discovery is limited to same-origin loopback development or the packaged native runtime, and private responses are not cached.

Third Parties

The app does not include advertising or analytics trackers. Optional notifications and consented workout upload use Vercel Functions and Upstash Redis; notifications also use Upstash QStash and the browser push service. Push endpoints and encryption keys are used only to deliver requested alerts. The workout endpoint is write-only and is not an account, cloud-history, or restore service.

Your Control

You can export your data, import a validated backup, revoke workout-upload consent, clear local data, or request deletion of this installation's remote records from Settings. Optional workout copies expire after at most 90 days. Turning cloud copy off immediately stops and clears pending uploads and requests deletion of retained copies; disabling notifications revokes push independently. Full remote deletion preserves local workouts and may continue through a visible retry state until bounded server cleanup is confirmed. Local clearing pauses while required deletion authorization or an unresolved active-timer cancellation must be retained. Exported app backups remain the recovery method.

Training Guidance

Progression recommendations are informational fitness guidance. The app does not diagnose medical conditions or provide medical treatment. Stop for severe, unexplained, or worsening pain and consult a qualified professional before making health or training decisions.
